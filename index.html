Hi, I’m Fariha Iffath

M.Sc at University of Calgary

Welcome to my website! I graduated in November 2024 with a Master's degree in Computer Science from the University of Calgary. Under the guidance of Dr. Marina Gavrilova, my research focused on human aesthetics for biometric identification. If you're interested in learning more about my research projects, feel free to visit my research page.

I am truly passionate about the captivating fields of computer vision, signal processing, natural language processing, deep learning, machine learning, and data analysis. Throughout my journey, I have dedicated a significant portion of my work to leveraging the potential of deep learning techniques in various computer vision tasks. However, I have also eagerly explored other exciting areas such as security and privacy, natural language processing, signal processing, and advanced data analysis methodologies. In my research, I utilize transfer learning models, meta-heuristic feature optimization techniques, deep fusion models, and data-driven insights to address complex challenges effectively.

News

  • September 2024: I received the *Graduate Students Award for Meritorious Research, Leadership, and Academic Excellence* from the University of Calgary, recognizing my contributions to research, leadership, and academic achievements.
  • July 2024: My paper titled *"ARF-Net: A Multi-Modal Aesthetic Attention-Based Fusion"* was accepted for presentation at the prestigious The Computer Graphics International Conference, held in Geneva, Switzerland. The paper was also accepted for fast-track journal publication. I had the privilege of presenting this work, highlighting advancements in multi-modal aesthetic fusion techniques.
  • May 2024: I represented the Biometric Lab at the "Get Science Done Strategic Plan Launch" in Calgary, Alberta. Purpose: To promote the lab’s research and engage audiences with innovative demonstrations. Responsibility: Organized and managed the lab’s booth, presented research highlights, and facilitated interactive simulations showcasing advancements in biometric research.
  • April 2024: My paper titled *"A Deep Learning Approach for Semantic Similarity Prediction Between Question Pairs Using Siamese Network and Word Embedding Techniques"* was accepted at the International Conference on Computing and Machine Intelligence, held in Michigan, USA. I presented the paper, showcasing a novel deep learning approach for predicting semantic similarity between question pairs.
